More UPrep planning

We will continue with our hypothetical problem that the UPrep staff gave us in Exercise 1.2.1. But the problem is evolving.

The UPrep staff love the fact that you were able to write a program to calculate the average number of students per grade for the the 2014/2015 school year. However, they would like you to also make these calculations for last year as well so they can compare the numbers from the two years to make sure their plan makes sense.

First, lets make a plan

Just like last time, we we'll create a plan for our problem by answering the following questions:

  • What is the input data?
  • What is the output data?
  • What are the steps in the algorithm?
  • What temporary data does the algorithm need?

What is the input data?

The UPrep staff have given us our input data for both this year year and last year:

What is the output data?

The output is two numbers:

  • The average students per grade for this year
  • The average students per grade for last year

What are the steps for the algorithm?

There are now two main steps in our algorithm, and each has two sub-steps:

  • For this year:

    • Calculate the total students for the school by adding together the number of students in each grade.
    • Calculate the average students per grade by dividing the total students by 7 (the number of grades in the school).

  • For last year:

    • Calculate the total students for the school by adding together the number of students in each grade.
    • Calculate the average students per grade by dividing the total students by 7 (the number of grades in the school).
